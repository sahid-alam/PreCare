01 — Process

Three steps,
five minutes, one clear answer.

No forms, no waiting rooms. Asha walks you through the same clinical intake a triage nurse would — just spoken out loud.

STEP / 01

Talk to Asha

Speak your symptoms naturally — “sore throat since Tuesday, fever this morning.” Asha asks the right follow-ups in plain language, never medical jargon.

STEP / 02

AI assessment

Symptoms are checked against clinical red-flag criteria in real time — vital signs, duration, severity, comorbidities — using protocols reviewed by physicians.

STEP / 03

Triage result

You get one of three clear recommendations — Home Care, Clinic Visit, or Emergency — with reasoning, next steps, and a printable summary for your doctor.

02 — Outcomes

Three outcomes. No ambiguity.

Every assessment ends in one of three colour-coded recommendations — the same triage categories used in hospital A&E rooms.

01 · Green

Home Care

Minor symptoms with no red-flags. Most people recover within 48 hours with self-care.

  • Rest, fluids, over-the-counter relief
  • Monitor for changes — Asha can check back in
  • Printable self-care plan for your fridge
Typical wait 0 min
02 · Amber

Clinic Visit

Moderate symptoms that warrant a doctor’s assessment, but aren’t time-critical.

  • See your GP within 24 hours
  • Auto-prepared symptom summary to share
  • List of nearby clinics with same-day slots
Recommended within 24 h
03 · Red

Emergency Room

High-risk symptoms detected. Time-critical care needed now — do not wait.

  • Call emergency services immediately (112)
  • Asha can dial for you and stay on the line
  • Your symptom log is sent ahead to the ER
Act NOW · CALL 112
Asha is a decision-support tool, not a substitute for professional medical care.  Always call your local emergency number in a life-threatening situation. PreCare does not diagnose, prescribe, or replace clinical judgement.
